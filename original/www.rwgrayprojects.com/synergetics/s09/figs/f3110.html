Fig. 931.10 Tetrahedral Characteristics of Chemical Bonding: Tetrahelix: Chemical bonds as demonstrated by arrangements of tetrahedra:
  1. The single-bonded tetrahedron system is like an engineering pin joint: it can move in any direction. It characterizes the behavior of gases.
  2. The double-bonded tetrahedron system is like and engineering hinge joint: it can rotate about an axis. It characterizes the behavior of liquids.
  3. The triple-bonded tetrahedron system is like and engineering fixed joint: it is rigid. It demonstrates the behavior of crystalline substances.
  4. & E. The quadri-bond and mid-edge-coordinate tetrahedron systems demonstrate the super strength of substances such as diamond and the metals.
  5. The tetrahelix: a helical array of triple-bonded tetrahedra.
