Combined VE & Icosahedron Great Circle Data


It is possible to fit an icosahedron inside a VE. The triangular faces of the icosahedron will then be skewed with respect to the triangular faces of the VE. The icosahedron face can be skewed (rotated) either clockwise or counter clockwise about the face-center-to-face-center axis of the icosahedron.

Consider a triangle and square face pair of the VE and two triangular faces of the icosahedron with their primary great circle arcs as shown below.

Next the icosahedron triangular face pair is rotated about an axis passing through the face center of the "top" icosahedron triangle. The rotation can be either clockwise or counter clockwise. These rotated pairs are then superimposed onto the VE triangle and square face pair matching up the triangular face center points. The icosahedron triangular faces are highlighted in a bold outline.

If you now look for the LCD spherical triangle of the VE you will see the new LCD spherical triangle for the combined VE and icosahedron system. This next illustration shows this LCD triangle with labels for the outer edge intersection points. Unfortunately, because of the size and quality of the image I don't think I can label all the inner intersection points. So you'll have to go to Fullr's book Synergetics 2 for the remainder of the labels.
